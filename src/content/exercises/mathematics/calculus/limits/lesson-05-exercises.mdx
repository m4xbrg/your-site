## Tier A – Foundational

1. Write out the ε–δ definition of:

$$
\lim_{x\to 2} (3x+1) = 7.
$$

2. Explain in your own words the meaning of:

$$
\forall \varepsilon > 0,\ \exists \delta > 0:\ 0<|x-a|<\delta \Rightarrow |f(x)-L|<\varepsilon.
$$

---

## Tier B – Mixed

3. For $ f(x) = 4x-5 $ and $ a = 1 $, compute $ L $ and propose a $\delta(\varepsilon)$ such that:

$$
0<|x-1|<\delta \Rightarrow |f(x)-L|<\varepsilon.
$$

4. Show that the constant function $ f(x)=2 $ satisfies the ε–δ definition for any point $ a $.  
   a. What choice of δ works for a given ε?  
   b. Why does this argument not depend on $ a $?

---

## Tier C – Challenge

5. Consider $ f(x) = x^2,\ a=2 $.

   a. Start from $ |x^2-4| $ and factor it into $ |x-2||x+2| $.  
   b. Show how to bound $ |x+2| $ by assuming a restriction on $ |x-2| $.  
   c. Derive an explicit $\delta(\varepsilon)$ that works.

6. Suppose both limits (in the ε–δ sense) hold:
$$
\lim_{x\to a} f(x)=L_1 \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x\to a} f(x)=L_2.
$$
   Use the definition to prove that $ L_1 = L_2 $.  
   *Hint:* Fix $\varepsilon$ and use both limits to constrain $ |L_1 - L_2| $.
