## Tier A – Foundational

1. Compute:

$$
\lim_{x\to 0^+} \frac{1}{x}.
$$

2. Compute:

$$
\lim_{x\to 0^-} \frac{1}{x}.
$$

3. Compute:

$$
\lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{3x^2 + 1}{6x^2 - 5}.
$$

---

## Tier B – Mixed

4. Determine:

$$
\lim_{x\to 2^-} \frac{|x-2|}{x-2}, \quad
\lim_{x\to 2^+} \frac{|x-2|}{x-2}.
$$

   a. Do the one-sided limits agree?  
   b. Does the two-sided limit exist?

5. Find all vertical asymptotes of:

$$
f(x) = \frac{x+1}{x^2 - 4}.
$$

   For each asymptote $ x=a $, determine the one-sided infinite limits.

6. Compute:

$$
\lim_{x\to -\infty} \frac{2x^3 - x}{-x^3 + 5}.
$$

   Explain how degree and leading coefficients determine the limit.

---

## Tier C – Challenge

7. Evaluate:

$$
\lim_{x\to 0} x\ln|x|.
$$

   *Hint:* analyze the behavior as $ x\to 0^- $ and $ x\to 0^+ $; consider $ |x| $ and compare growth rates.

8. Let:

$$
f(x)=\frac{1}{(x-1)^2}.
$$

   a. Compute $ \lim_{x\to1^-} f(x) $ and $ \lim_{x\to1^+} f(x) $.  
   b. Classify the discontinuity at $ x=1 $ using the language of infinite limits.  
   c. Explain why this discontinuity is not removable.
